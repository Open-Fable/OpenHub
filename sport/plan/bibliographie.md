# Bibliographie Scientifique et Sources de Référence

Ce document répertorie les sources fondamentales utilisées pour l'élaboration du guide "NutriBody". Les sources sont classées par thématique correspondant aux chapitres.

## 1. Balance Énergétique et Composition Corporelle

- **Hall, K. D., & Guo, J. (2017).** _Maximal weight loss rates in obesity._ International Journal of Obesity. (Étude sur les limites thermodynamiques de la perte de gras).
- **Strasser, B., et al. (2015).** _Fat loss depends on energy deficit only, independently of the method for weight loss._ Annals of Nutrition and Metabolism.
- **Huovinen, V., et al. (2015).** _Overfeeding in young adults._ (Sur le stockage des graisses vs gain musculaire lors d'un surplus).

## 2. Protéines et Hypertrophie

- **Morton, R. W., et al. (2018).** _A systematic review, meta-analysis and meta-regression of the effect of protein supplementation on resistance training-induced gains in muscle mass and strength in healthy adults._ British Journal of Sports Medicine. (La source de référence pour le chiffre de 1,6g/kg).
- **Schoenfeld, B. J., & Aragon, A. A. (2018).** _How much protein can the body use in a single meal for muscle-building? Implications for daily protein distribution._ Journal of the International Society of Sports Nutrition.
- **Phillips, S. M. (2014).** _A brief review of critical processes in exercising human skeletal muscle._ Sports Medicine.

## 3. Glucides, Lipides et Performance

- **Burke, L. M., et al. (2011).** _Carbohydrates for training and competition._ Journal of Sports Sciences.
- **Sallinen, J., et al. (2004).** _Relationship between diet and serum hormone concentrations during prolonged training in men._ (Impact des lipides sur la testostérone).
- **Aragon, A. A., et al. (2017).** _International Society of Sports Nutrition position stand: diets and body composition._ JISSN.

## 4. Micronutriments et Santé du Sportif

- **Larson-Meyer, D. E., & Willis, K. S. (2010).** _Vitamin D and athletes._ Current Sports Medicine Reports.
- **Volpe, S. L. (2015).** _Magnesium and the Athlete._ ACSM's Health & Fitness Journal.
- **Slavin, J. (2008).** _Health benefits of fruits and vegetables are from additive and synergistic combinations of phytochemicals._ American Journal of Clinical Nutrition.

## 5. Hydratation

- **Sawka, M. N., et al. (2007).** _American College of Sports Medicine position stand. Exercise and fluid replacement._ Medicine & Science in Sports & Exercise.
- **Thomas, D. T., et al. (2016).** _American College of Sports Medicine Joint Position Statement. Nutrition and Athletic Performance._ Medicine & Science in Sports & Exercise.

## 6. Timing Nutritionnel

- **Aragon, A. A., & Schoenfeld, B. J. (2013).** _Nutrient timing revisited: is there a post-exercise anabolic window?_ Journal of the International Society of Sports Nutrition.
- **Jäger, R., et al. (2017).** _International Society of Sports Nutrition Position Stand: protein and exercise._ JISSN. (Sur la distribution des protéines).

## 7. Compléments Alimentaires

- **Buford, T. W., et al. (2007).** _International Society of Sports Nutrition position stand: creatine supplementation and exercise._ JISSN.
- **Goldstein, E. R., et al. (2010).** _International Society of Sports Nutrition position stand: caffeine and performance._ JISSN.
- **Maughan, R. J., et al. (2018).** _IOC consensus statement: dietary supplements and the high-performance athlete._ British Journal of Sports Medicine.

## 8. Psychologie et Adhérence

- **Helms, E. R., et al. (2014).** _Evidence-based recommendations for natural bodybuilding contest preparation: nutrition and supplementation._ Journal of the International Society of Sports Nutrition.
- **Stewart, T. M., et al. (2002).** _Rigid vs. flexible dieting: association with eating disorder symptoms in nonobese women._ Appetite. (Base scientifique pour le "Flexible Dieting").

## Ouvrages de Référence Généraux

- **Helms, E., Valdez, A., & Morgan, A.** _The Muscle & Strength Pyramids - Nutrition._ (Considéré comme la "bible" de la nutrition basée sur la science).
- **Israetel, M., et al.** _The Renaissance Diet 2.0._ Renaissance Periodization.
